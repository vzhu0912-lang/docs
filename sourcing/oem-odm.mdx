## OEM vs. ODM: What's the Difference?

**OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer)** means Topleo manufactures an existing, proven projector platform and applies your brand identity — logo, color scheme, packaging design, and any required market certifications — so the finished product ships as your own branded product.

**ODM (Original Design Manufacturer)** goes further: Topleo's engineering team works from your custom specifications to design and manufacture a projector from the ground up. You define the chassis form factor, display technology, software features, and UI; Topleo's engineers bring it to life.

## OEM / ODM Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit Your Requirements">
    Share your project brief with the Topleo sourcing team — include target model (for OEM) or product specifications (for ODM), branding assets, desired certifications, target market, and anticipated order quantities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Topleo Prepares a Sample">
    For OEM orders, a PCBA sample is ready within **24 hours**. For ODM projects, the engineering team scopes a development timeline and delivers a prototype based on your agreed specification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sample Review & Approval">
    You evaluate the sample against your requirements. Topleo's team addresses any revision requests quickly, iterating until the product meets your exact standards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mass Production">
    Once you approve the sample and confirm your purchase order, Topleo moves into mass production. Standard production runs are completed in **7 working days**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Shipping (DDP / DDU)">
    Finished units ship from Shenzhen or from Topleo's regional warehouses (US and Poland). Choose DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) for a fully landed price, or DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid) for flexibility on your end.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Minimum order quantities vary by model and customization scope. Contact the Topleo sourcing team for MOQ details specific to your project.
</Note>
